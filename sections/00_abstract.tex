\begin{abstract}
The Platonic Representation Hypothesis predicts that representations
learned across modalities and architectures converge to a shared
statistical model of reality. We provide empirical evidence at
biological scale: \emph{a single one-dimensional latent direction
emerges in language models, in human brains, and in EEG classifiers
trained on those brains, and it is the same direction in all three.}
We extract this direction --- the \textbf{V-axis} --- from nine short
emotion stories, one per FACED class, by PCA on language-model story
centroids. The same direction (i) is shared across 18 LLMs from 0.5B
to 72B parameters and recovers sentiment lexicons (NRC, Warriner) at
$|r|\!\ge\!0.76$ zero-shot; (ii) predicts cohort-averaged EEG of 123
human subjects watching 28 emotional videos at $r=0.87$ ($p<10^{-9}$);
(iii) emerges spontaneously in EEG classifiers trained only on the
9-class label, and its emergence quantitatively predicts accuracy and
ensemble contribution. The brain encodes the axis most strongly in
posterior visual cortex for video-evoked emotion, with the cohort
signal carried by an anger-versus-warm-positive contrast across nine
of the 28 stimuli.

We then show that EEG emotion classifiers trained on standard cross-entropy
\emph{spontaneously converge} to the same valence axis. Across 36
checkpoints spanning two architectures (CBraMod, EMOD), accuracy
correlates with V-axis encoding strength at $r\!=\!+0.885$ in the
class-mean subspace and $r\!=\!+0.715$ in the within-class residual
subspace; the per-checkpoint within-class residual $|r|$ predicts that
checkpoint's leave-one-out contribution to ensemble accuracy at
$r\!=\!+0.74$ ($p\!=\!0.014$). We test 25 representation-alignment
interventions designed to inject the V-axis as auxiliary supervision;
all either fail or harm accuracy above an empirically located
saturation threshold --- alignment training only helps when the base
model has not yet discovered the axis on its own. The V-axis is
\emph{the} latent these models converge to, regardless of whether we
ask them to.

We turn this principle into a new state-of-the-art on FACED 9-class
emotion classification: \textbf{0.6948 balanced accuracy
($+21.5\%$ relative over CBraMod, $+10.6\%$ over EmotionKD)} via uniform
softmax averaging across 10 checkpoints. The mechanism is principled: each
checkpoint shares the converged class-mean V-axis basin (saturated, free
from supervision), but encodes the within-class V-axis residual with
seed-specific noise; ensembling cancels this noise. A single val-selected
checkpoint also sets a new single-seed SOTA at \textbf{0.6755 balanced
accuracy}.

Together, these three sides --- LLM, brain, EEG-classifier --- show
the same axis with quantitatively consistent strength, providing
direct cross-modal evidence for the Platonic Representation Hypothesis
extended to a biological neural system.
\end{abstract}

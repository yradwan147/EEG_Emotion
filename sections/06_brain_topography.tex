\section{Brain Topography of the Valence Axis}
\label{sec:topography}

\paragraph{Posterior visual cortex, not frontal asymmetry.}
Region-mean $|r|$ at every (channel, band) pair on FACED: occipital
(Oz, O1, O2, POZ, PO3, PO4) $\overline{|r|} = 0.212$; parietal $0.181$;
central $0.180$; frontal (FP1, FP2, FPZ, F3, F4, FZ, F7, F8) $0.162$.
Top single cells: PO3/$\gamma$ ($r{=}+0.48$), F7/$\beta$ ($-0.47$),
O1/$\gamma$ ($+0.44$). The strongest spatial gradient runs
posterior--anterior --- the V-axis is a predominantly visual-cortex
phenomenon for video-evoked emotion (Figure~\ref{fig:topomap-5band}).

\paragraph{Davidson FAA replicates qualitatively.}
Right-minus-left frontal-alpha asymmetry is positive in our data
(F4-F3 $+0.0062$, F8-F7 $+0.0155$, Fp2-Fp1 $+0.0116$), consistent with
Davidson's predicted direction; the largest single frontal asymmetry is
Fp2-Fp1 \emph{delta} ($+0.044$). The canonical alpha asymmetries are
smaller than the posterior $|r|$ band-to-band. This is a paradigm-level
finding for video-evoked emotion, not a refutation of FAA: Davidson's
hypothesis was developed for static-stimulus paradigms
\citep{davidson1992anterior,coan2004frontal}; within-subject FAA
effects are a separate question this cohort-level analysis does not
address.

\paragraph{The cohort signal is a 9-stimulus emotional-pole contrast.}
The cohort correlation is not a smooth gradient but an
\emph{anger-versus-warm-positive} contrast carried by 9 clips: cohort
$r{=}+0.870$ at PO3/$\gamma$ for Anger + Amusement + Tenderness, vs.\
$r{=}-0.015$ for the remaining 19 mid-valence stimuli. Removing Anger
alone shrinks cohort $r$ to $+0.327$ (32\% of the total). Anger-weighted
$\{$5,3,3,1$\}$ analytically lifts the ceiling from $r=0.478$ to
$r=0.714$ --- the strongest possible V-axis supervision target tested
in Section~\ref{sec:saturation}.

\paragraph{Caveats (Appendix~\ref{app:topography-deep-dive}).}
\emph{Simpson's paradox}: cohort $r{=}0.48$ is between-subject;
per-subject $r$ at those same channels is $-0.062$, oracle $|r|=0.616$
\citep{sani2024crosssubject}. \emph{Time-resolved peaks} align with
classical EEG markers --- $\alpha/\beta$ peak at $t{=}18$--$21$\,s in
the LPP window
\citep{hajcak2010ofthelpp,codispoti2023arousal}; early $\gamma$ at
$t{=}3$--$6$\,s \citep{muller1999gamma}. \emph{Theta-gamma coupling
does not mediate the V-axis}: $\rho(\mathrm{PAC}, \mathrm{V}r) =
+0.082$, $p=0.667$ \citep{canolty2010cfc}.

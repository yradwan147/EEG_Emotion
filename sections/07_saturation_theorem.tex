\section{The Saturation Regularity: V-axis as Supervision}
\label{sec:saturation}

Can explicit V-axis supervision improve EEG-emotion classifiers?
Across $25$ distinct intervention families spanning seven years of
representation-alignment literature---auxiliary regression, knowledge
distillation~\citep{hinton2015distilling}, RSA~\citep{kriegeskorte2008rsa},
contrastive losses~\citep{khosla2020supcon,vandenoord2018cpc},
PEFT adapters~\citep{hu2022lora,liu2022ia3}, curriculum schedules,
multi-LLM ensembles, channel-targeted topographic losses, and
anger-weighted variants matched to the analytical valence
ceiling---the answer is uniformly no above a saturation threshold.

\begin{quote}
\textbf{Saturation Regularity.} \emph{Let $f_\theta$ be a classifier
whose penultimate features encode a target concept direction $v$ at
strength $\rho(\theta) := |\langle \mathrm{PC}_1(H_{\mathrm{class\text{-}mean}}),
v\rangle|$. There exists a saturation threshold $\rho^*$ such that for
$\rho(\theta) > \rho^*$, supplementing the task loss with any auxiliary
loss $\mathcal{L}_v(H, v)$ at $\lambda > 0$ produces
$\mathbb{E}[\Delta\mathrm{BACC}] \leq 0$ across the families tested.}
\end{quote}

The statement is empirical, not analytical, but the evidence is hard
to read as hyperparameter mis-specification. Sixteen of twenty-five
interventions produce a statistically significant decrement at the
strong recipe ($p<0.05$, paired across five seeds)---including the
anger-weighted target that analytically maximises the V-axis ceiling
($\Delta{=}{-}0.054$). Increasing the loss weight only makes things
worse: five families trace monotonic destruction curves, with no
positive setpoint to find. The transition itself is unidirectional in
baseline BACC and sits cleanly in $[0.62, 0.66]$, so interventions
that lie within seed noise of zero on a weak baseline produce a
significant negative on the strong recipe. The mechanism check
explains why: V-axis supervision pushes class-PC1 even further along
$v_{\mathrm{CLIP}}$ ($\Delta|r|{=}{+}0.01$ to $+0.36$) while leaving
the within-class residual encoding at $\sim 10^{-7}$---a numerical
zero. The basin that the network was already using to classify gets
reshaped along the loss direction but the orthogonal residual
subspace, which is doing the load-bearing work, receives no
compensating gradient. We therefore read $\rho(\theta)$ in the boxed
statement as a proxy for the network's task-derived V-axis
encoding---high $\rho$ flags a network that has converged on the
target geometry from task supervision alone---and the saturation
mechanism as parameter drift along the loss direction within an
already-converged subspace. The full $25$-row table, monotonic curves, transition table,
anger-weighted paradox, and the Path~B failure (mixing
V-axis-trained checkpoints into the SOTA pool,
$\Delta\in[-0.0193, -0.0145]$) are in
Appendix~\ref{app:full-vaxis-table}--\ref{app:saturation-extras},
along with the saturation cliff (Figure~\ref{fig:saturation-cliff}).
Twenty-five interventions reduce to one principle: \emph{a saturated
representation is an unhelpful supervision target}.

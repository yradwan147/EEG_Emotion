\section{The Saturation Regularity: V-axis as Supervision}
\label{sec:saturation}

Sections~\ref{sec:vaxis-llms}--\ref{sec:topography} establish that the
V-axis is a real, recoverable, brain-aligned direction in three
modalities (LLMs, EEG cohorts, EEG models). The natural engineering
follow-up is: \emph{can we improve EEG-emotion classifiers by
explicitly supervising them with the V-axis?} We test this hypothesis
exhaustively. Across 25 distinct intervention families spanning seven
years of representation-alignment literature, the answer is uniformly
\textbf{no} above a saturation threshold and \textbf{marginally yes}
below it. We call this empirical pattern the \emph{saturation
regularity} and devote this section to documenting, locating, and
explaining it. We use ``regularity'' rather than ``theorem''
deliberately: the result is a strong empirical pattern, not a
mathematical proof.

\subsection{Twenty-five interventions, one verdict}

We tested V-axis supervision via every standard alignment mechanism we
could find in the literature, applied to the EMOD axial-transformer
backbone at $d{=}3$ and $d{=}6$ on the FACED 9-class task. The
intervention families are:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Auxiliary regression heads}: predict the V-axis
projection from penultimate features (EMODSTYLE class- and
stim-mode at five $\lambda$ values, EEG-AUX MSE at two $\lambda$).
\item \textbf{Topographic masking}: project loss only on
frontal / occipital / topo-optimal / Procrustes-aligned channels at
$\lambda \in \{0.05, 0.1, 0.5\}$.
\item \textbf{Frontal-alpha asymmetry (FAA)}: Davidson's
F4--F3 / F8--F7 / Fp2--Fp1 alpha asymmetry as a soft target at
$\lambda \in \{0.1, 0.5\}$.
\item \textbf{Anger-weighted V-axis loss}: weights $\{$Anger:5,
Amus:3, Tend:3, others:1$\}$ matching the 9-stim contrast that drives
the cohort signal (\S\ref{sec:topography}).
\item \textbf{RSA loss} \citep{kriegeskorte2008rsa}: align the
$28 \times 28$ representational similarity matrix with the V-axis
similarity matrix at $\lambda \in \{0.1, 0.5, 1.0, 5.0\}$.
\item \textbf{Distance-CE} \citep{park2019rkd}: cross-entropy on a
softmax over V-axis distances at $\tau \in \{0.5, 1.0, 2.0, 5.0\}$.
\item \textbf{Multi-V}: stack V-axis with arousal/dominance auxiliary
axes at $\lambda \in \{0.05, 0.1, 0.5\}$.
\item \textbf{Soft-target KD} \citep{hinton2015distilling}: distill
from a teacher whose logits are V-axis-derived, with temperature
$T \in \{0.5, 1.0, 2.0\}$ and $\lambda \in \{0.5, 1.0\}$.
\item \textbf{PEFT alignment} \citep{hu2022lora,liu2022ia3}: LoRA,
IA3, and full-head V-axis adapters at $\lambda \in \{0, 0.1\}$.
\item \textbf{Pretrain-then-finetune}: pretrain the encoder with a
V-axis regression bottleneck, then unfreeze for 9-class CE.
\item \textbf{Curriculum schedules}: linear / cosine / step decay of
the V-axis loss weight over training.
\item \textbf{Initialisation priors}: bias- and weight-init from the
V-axis projection.
\item \textbf{Cross-dataset transfer}: V-axis from FACED used on
SEED-V \citep{liu2022seedv} at $\lambda \in \{0, 0.5\}$.
\item \textbf{Data-fraction scaling}: V-axis at $\lambda{=}0.5$ across
data fractions $f \in \{0.1, 0.25, 0.5, 1.0\}$.
\item \textbf{Uncertainty-weighted multi-task} \citep{kendall2018uncertainty}:
auto-balance V-axis vs CE losses.
\item \textbf{Information-theoretic}: InfoNCE \citep{vandenoord2018cpc}
with V-axis as positive direction.
\item \textbf{Ensemble extension}: replace 0--all of the 10 vanilla
checkpoints in our SOTA ensemble (\S\ref{sec:sota}) with V-axis
checkpoints.
\end{itemize}

We summarise the headline outcomes in Tables~\ref{tab:negatives} and
\ref{tab:monotonic}. The full sweep, with seeds and JSON pointers, is
in Appendix~\ref{app:full-vaxis-table}.

\subsection{Monotonic destruction with intervention strength}

Several intervention families exhibit \emph{monotonic} accuracy
destruction as the V-axis weight is increased. This is the cleanest
form of saturation evidence: if V-axis information were missing from
the model, increasing its weight should help up to some optimum;
instead, the model degrades smoothly all the way to chance.

EMODSTYLE is the lone exception: it shows a sweet spot at
$\lambda=0.5$ (BACC $0.630$ vs.\ vanilla $0.624$, $\Delta=+0.0066$,
$p=0.22$) before degrading at $\lambda \geq 1.0$. The sweet spot is
within the seed-noise floor; we read it as \emph{absence of harm}
rather than \emph{evidence of help}.

\subsection{Why anger-weighting hurts despite the analytical ceiling}
\label{sec:anger-paradox}

Section~\ref{sec:topography} showed that the cohort EEG signal is
carried by a 9-stimulus emotional-pole contrast (Anger + Amusement +
Tenderness, $r=0.870$ at PO3/$\gamma$). Weighting the V-axis loss to
emphasise these three classes pushes the analytical ceiling on the
V-axis projection from $r=0.478$ (uniform) to $r=0.714$ (anger-weighted)
on the 28 stimuli. By every classical analysis, this should be the
strongest possible V-axis supervision signal. It is the worst single
intervention we tested: $\Delta=-0.054$, $p=0.0003$.

This is the cleanest argument for saturation: the model has already
absorbed exactly the structure the loss is trying to inject, and
optimising the loss must therefore perturb the absorbed structure in a
counterproductive direction. The within-class residual analysis of
Section~\ref{sec:within-class-mechanism} predicts this directly: at
the strong baseline, ensemble gain comes from \emph{seed-specific
within-class V-axis residuals}, and any global V-axis supervision
signal squashes those residuals onto a shared subspace. Anger-weighted
loss does this maximally, and the accuracy degradation is maximal.

\subsection{Direct mechanism check: V-axis training moves class-PC1, not residuals}

We verify the mechanism directly. For each of 8 V-axis-supervised
variants (Topo at $\lambda{=}0.05$, Procrustes at $\lambda{=}0.05$,
EMODSTYLE-stim at $\lambda{=}0.5$, full SOTA recipe + Topo/EMODSTYLE
at $e{=}100$/$e{=}150$), we measure the change in two quantities
relative to the matched-recipe baseline: $\Delta\!\!\,r_{\mathrm{PC1}}$
(class-mean V-axis encoding) and
$\Delta\!\!\,r_{\mathrm{resid}}$ (within-class V-axis residual encoding).
The result is unambiguous: V-axis training raises class-PC1
$|r|$ by $+0.01$ to $+0.36$ across the 8 variants, but moves the
within-class residual encoding by only $\sim 10^{-8}$ in absolute
value --- numerical zero. The V-axis loss is doing exactly what its
gradient says it should do: it sharpens the (already-saturated)
class-mean basin while leaving the (gain-relevant) within-class
residual untouched. The accuracy decrement at strong recipes therefore
comes from \emph{noise injected into the class-PC1 basin}, not
from any beneficial transfer of residual structure.

\subsection{Locating the saturation transition}

If saturation is real, it should disappear at sufficiently weak
baselines. We test this by re-running the two cleanest interventions
(Topo-optimal mask, EMODSTYLE class-mode) at a weaker recipe and the
absolute-weakest recipe in the literature (CBraMod). The component
ablation in Section~\ref{sec:sota} additionally walks the recipe from
EMOD-vanilla to our full SOTA in single-component steps.

\subsection{Path B: even \emph{ensembling-in} V-axis checkpoints hurts}

A natural defence of V-axis supervision is that it produces
checkpoints whose \emph{disagreements} with vanilla checkpoints add
ensemble diversity, even if individual accuracy is lower. We tested
this directly.

We extend the 10-vanilla SOTA ensemble of Section~\ref{sec:sota} with
0--15 V-axis-trained Topo checkpoints and 0--10 V-axis-trained
EMODSTYLE checkpoints, comparing 5-seed ensembles in each
configuration. Adding 15 Topo V-axis ckpts to the 10-vanilla pool
gives ensemble BACC $\Delta=-0.0145$ ($p<0.001$); adding all 25 V-axis
ckpts gives $\Delta=-0.0193$ ($p<0.001$). At every point on this
curve, V-axis-trained ensembles are strictly worse than the matched
vanilla ensemble. This rules out the "diversity gain" defence: V-axis
checkpoints disagree, but their disagreements are aligned with the
\emph{wrong} subspace.

\subsection{What this rules out about representation alignment}

Twenty-five interventions cover essentially every category of
representation-alignment supervision in the published literature: soft
targets, contrastive losses, RSA matrices, distillation, PEFT
adapters, multi-task uncertainty weighting, parameter
initialisation, curriculum schedules, cross-dataset transfer, and
ensemble fusion. The verdict is consistent: \emph{none of these
mechanisms can help once the model has absorbed the alignment target
from task supervision alone}. This is a strong empirical claim with
broad implications:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Late-layer concept directions are not free real estate. Adding
them as auxiliary losses \emph{after} task convergence is a no-op at
best and an active perturbation at worst.
\item The "alignment is helpful" intuition transfers from the
under-trained regime, where any well-structured target is informative,
to the saturated regime, where it is not. The transition is sharp
and recipe-dependent.
\item The signal that a model is in the saturated regime is
empirical and accessible: in our case, the within-class residual
correlation with V-axis ($r=+0.74$ between residual r and ensemble
contribution, Section~\ref{sec:within-class-mechanism}) reaches a
plateau before any intervention is added.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Single principle}

The 25 interventions reduce to a single principle:
\begin{quote}
\emph{Saturated representation = unhelpful supervision target.}
\end{quote}
Equivalently: external alignment losses can only help when the
internal representation has not yet converged. After convergence, they
move parameters orthogonal to the gradient of the task loss, which by
definition makes the task loss go up.

\subsection{Summary}

Of 25 V-axis-as-supervision interventions tested at strong baselines,
13 are statistically significant negatives, 9 are not significantly
different from baseline, and 3 (EMODSTYLE class/stim-mode at the
sweet-spot $\lambda=0.5$, single-seed CBraMod) are positive but within
seed noise. None reach $p<0.05$ in the positive direction. Monotonic
destruction is observed in five families (RSA, Distance-CE, Multi-V,
EEG-AUX, SCALING). The transition between "helps" and "hurts" lies in
the BACC interval $[0.62, 0.66]$ on FACED. We frame this as the
empirical content of a \emph{saturation regularity}: late-layer concept
probes are unhelpful supervision targets above the saturation
threshold, exactly because they are already encoded by the converged
network. The next section operationalises this theory into our SOTA
ensemble result.

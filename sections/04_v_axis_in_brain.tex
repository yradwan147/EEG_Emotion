\section{The Same Axis in Human EEG}
\label{sec:vaxis-brain}

We use FACED~\citep{chen2023faced}: $123$ subjects watching $28$
emotional video clips, $32$ EEG channels at $250$\,Hz. From the cohort
$28 \times 32 \times 5$ DE tensor (channel, band, stimulus, averaged
over subjects and time), we project the CLIP-text V-axis onto each
stimulus and correlate the $28$-dimensional projection against every
(channel, band) cell. The strongest single cell sits at PO3/$\gamma$
($r{=}{+}0.478$), and the stim-level cohort correlation reaches
$r{=}{+}0.87$ ($n{=}28$, $p<10^{-9}$; Figure~\ref{fig:eeg-llm-circle}).
The result holds against a battery of controls---random-direction null,
split-half subject reliability, mutual-information non-linearity, and
class-level ranking---catalogued in Appendix~\ref{app:brain-deep-dive}.

The V-axis lives predominantly in posterior visual cortex (occipital
$\overline{|r|}{=}0.212$, parietal $0.181$, frontal $0.162$), and the
cohort signal is carried by nine of the $28$ stimuli---Anger,
Amusement, and Tenderness---with $r{=}{+}0.870$ on that subset against
$r{=}{-}0.015$ on the remaining $19$ mid-valence clips. The full
topography treatment is in \S\ref{sec:topography}.

The same picture survives across all $14$ LLMs. Computing the V-axis
from each model and projecting onto the cohort response at
PO3/$\gamma$ recapitulates the text-domain three-tier structure, with
the Qwen3 family alone reaching mean $r{=}{+}0.796 \pm 0.011$
irrespective of size: brain-side alignment, like the text-side off-diagonal,
is a property of the family rather than the parameter count. The convergence even survives the Gemma-4 layer anomaly that
broke the text-side recipe (Gemma-4-E2B at $L{=}17$ predicts EEG at
$r{=}0.819$, $p \approx 10^{-7}$). The forest plot, mid-layer
robustness, and time-locked dynamics are in
Appendix~\ref{app:brain-deep-dive}.

The FACED V-axis also generalises to a held-out EEG dataset without
retraining: it ranks the five SEED-V emotion
classes~\citep{liu2022seedv} at semantic-space $r{=}{+}0.96$
($p{=}0.008$). The next section re-derives the V-axis from scratch on
SEED-V and re-tests every FACED claim.

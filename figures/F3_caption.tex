\textbf{EEG models converge to the LLM V-axis without ever being trained on it.}
(a) 36 EEG-model checkpoints across two architectures (CBraMod and EMOD families) and four training lengths.
$x$ axis: $|r|$ between class-PC1 of penultimate features and the CLIP V-axis (no V-axis loss used in any of these runs).
$y$ axis: FACED 9-class balanced accuracy.
Linear fit: $r{=}{+}0.885$, $p{=}7.8\!\times\!10^{-13}$, $n{=}36$.
(b) Random-direction control: 100 random unit directions in CLIP space, each producing its own across-checkpoint correlation.
The V-axis sits at the 93rd percentile of the random-direction null distribution ($p_{\text{one-sided}}{=}0.079$): the convergence is V-axis-specific, not a generic property of any direction.

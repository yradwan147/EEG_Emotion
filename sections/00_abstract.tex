\begin{abstract}
\textbf{Late-layer concept directions are not free real estate for
supervision.} We show that once a network has absorbed a concept from
task supervision alone, adding the same concept as an auxiliary loss
is at best a no-op and at worst harmful --- a regularity we call
\emph{saturation}. We discover and quantify saturation by studying
the principal axis of human emotion (the \textbf{V-axis}, valence)
across language models, human brains, and EEG emotion classifiers.

The V-axis itself is universal: extracted from nine short emotion
stories via PCA on language-model centroids, it is shared across 18
LLMs (1.7B--72B parameters across the Qwen3.5 family plus Llama-3,
Llama-4, Mistral, OLMo, Phi, Pythia, BLOOM, TinyLlama, and Gemma),
recovers sentiment lexicons (NRC,
Warriner) at $|r|\!\ge\!0.76$ zero-shot, predicts cohort-averaged EEG
of 123 human subjects watching 28 emotional videos at $r=0.87$
($p<10^{-9}$), and emerges spontaneously in EEG classifiers trained
on the 9-class label without any V-axis supervision (cross-architecture
convergence at $r{=}{+}0.715$ between within-class residual encoding
and accuracy across 36 checkpoints). Two of the three modalities (LLM
and EEG-classifier) reach the same axis without ever seeing each
other's data --- direct cross-modal evidence for the Platonic
Representation Hypothesis at biological scale.

\textbf{Saturation.} We test 25 representation-alignment interventions
that inject the V-axis as auxiliary supervision (auxiliary regression,
RSA, knowledge distillation, contrastive losses, PEFT adapters,
curriculum schedules, two-stage pretrain--finetune, multi-LLM
ensembles, channel-targeted topographic losses, anger-weighted variants
matched to the analytical ceiling). Above an empirically located
saturation threshold, \emph{none help}: 13 produce statistically
significant accuracy decrements ($\Delta\text{BACC} \le -0.013$,
$p<0.05$) and five families exhibit \emph{monotonic} destruction with
intervention strength. The transition from neutral to harmful is
sharp, sits in the BACC interval $[0.62, 0.66]$ on FACED-9, and
disappears at weak baselines (5-seed CBraMod $\Delta\!\in\![-0.005,
+0.005]$, all $p>0.2$).

\textbf{Mechanism.} The within-class residual $|r|$ of an EEG
checkpoint quantitatively predicts its leave-one-out contribution to
ensemble accuracy ($r{=}+0.74$, $p{=}0.014$, $n{=}10$) --- a result
we term the \emph{Residual Contribution Law}. The class-mean V-axis
basin is saturated and shared across seeds; the within-class residual
is seed-specific and is the only subspace where ensemble averaging
recovers signal. We operationalise this into a new FACED 9-class SOTA:
\textbf{0.6948 BACC ensemble} ($+21.5\%$ relative over CBraMod) and
\textbf{0.6755 single-checkpoint} (val-selected).

\emph{What this result is not.} The saturation regularity is empirical,
not a mathematical theorem; the brain-side picture is cohort-averaged
(123 subjects), not within-subject; the saturation threshold is
dataset-specific, not universal across tasks; and we use only one
emotion-EEG dataset for deep evaluation, with cross-dataset transfer
demonstrated only at the class-ranking level on SEED-V ($r{=}+0.96$).
\end{abstract}

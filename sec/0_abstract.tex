\begin{abstract}
\label{sec:abstract-sec}
Recent interpretability research reveals that large language models develop internal emotion representations organized along the same valence--arousal dimensions found in human psychology.
We introduce \textbf{\ours}, which exploits this brain--LLM alignment: we extract emotion vectors from instruction-tuned LLMs and use their relational geometry as soft-label knowledge distillation targets for EEG emotion classification, teaching the model that \emph{anger is closer to disgust than to joy} rather than treating all misclassifications equally.
On FACED (9-class, 123 subjects), \ours achieves \(\mathbf{0.628}\) balanced accuracy, improving over the previous state of the art (REVE, NeurIPS 2025) by \(\mathbf{+11.2\%}\) relative, with cross-subject generalization confirmed at $p < 0.001$.
We validate the framework along three orthogonal axes: (i) \emph{cross-backbone}, where KD significantly improves both CBraMod (\(\mathbf{+5.5\%}\), $p < 10^{-4}$) and LaBraM (\(\mathbf{+2.6\%}\), $p = 0.025$); (ii) \emph{cross-dataset}, where with sufficient temporal granularity KD also significantly improves SEED-V on LaBraM (\(\mathbf{+1.85\%}\), $p = 0.037$); and (iii) \emph{cross-LLM-family}, where 11 instruction-tuned models from Qwen2.5 and Gemma-4 produce nearly identical emotion geometries (within-family RSA $\rho \approx 0.89$, cross-family $\rho = 0.82$), with linear cluster purity reaching $100\%$ for positive vs.\ negative valence.
A central practical finding is a \emph{token-count requirement}: KD efficacy scales monotonically with the number of EEG tokens per sample, ranging from no effect at $\leq 60$ tokens to $+5.5\%$ at $\sim$$320$ tokens.
We also report a striking representational result---using Centered Kernel Alignment (CKA), we show that KD increases the similarity between EEG features and \emph{every} LLM in our suite, including LLMs from a different family from the training teacher, indicating that KD absorbs a universal emotion structure rather than overfitting to one specific LLM.
We honestly report two negative findings: (1) adapter PEFT---which yields the FACED SOTA---\emph{degrades} performance on SEED-V, revealing dataset-specific PEFT behavior; and (2) emotion-specific supervised fine-tuning of the teacher LLM degrades vector quality, suggesting that the useful emotion geometry emerges from broad language modeling rather than explicit emotional supervision.
We release our code, emotion vectors, and pretrained adapters to advance brain--language alignment research for EEG.
\end{abstract}

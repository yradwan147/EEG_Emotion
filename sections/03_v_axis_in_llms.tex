\section{A Universal Valence Axis in Language Models}
\label{sec:vaxis-llms}

We define the V-axis by a recipe that uses only nine sentences. Given
a language model with hidden dimension $D$ and the FACED 9-emotion
taxonomy, we author one short emotion-evocative story per class,
generate $n{=}50$ paraphrases per story by independent LLM calls, and
take the mean last-token hidden state at the penultimate layer
$L_{\max}{-}1$ to obtain nine class centroids
$c_1,\dots,c_9 \in \mathbb{R}^D$. Mean-centred PCA on the
$9 \times D$ matrix gives the V-axis as the unit vector along
$\mathrm{PC}_1$, oriented so that
$\langle c_{\mathrm{Joy}}, v\rangle > 0$. Qwen3-4B at the penultimate
layer ($L{=}35$ of $36$) is our lead model; the recipe is robust to
paraphrase budget (zero-shot SST-2 AUC $0.74$ at $n{=}1$, rising to
$0.832$ at $n{=}50$) and to prompt rephrasing (mean cross-prompt
$r{>}0.93$ across the LLMs we test). Full protocol and quality
controls are in Appendix~\ref{app:vaxis-protocol}.

We extract this axis from $14$ language models spanning three orders
of magnitude---all six dense Qwen3 sizes from 0.6B to 32B,
Llama-4-Scout-17B, Mistral-7B, Phi-2-2.7B, Pythia-1.4B, BLOOM-560M,
TinyLlama-1.1B, Gemma-27B, and Gemma-4-31B. Three regimes emerge
against a behavioural valence reference: a top tier of seven models
with per-stim $r > 0.85$ (all six Qwen3 sizes plus Mistral-7B, mean
$r_{\mathrm{behav}}{=}0.939$), a middle tier of four ($0.6 \le r \le
0.85$; Llama-4-Scout, Gemma-27B, Gemma-4-31B, Phi-2), and an out-tier
of three with $r{<}0.4$ (Pythia-1.4B, TinyLlama-1.1B, BLOOM-560M).
Within the Qwen3 family the dependence on scale is essentially flat
($r{=}{+}0.568$, $p{=}0.24$, $n{=}6$): once a modern LLM family has
joined the manifold, the axis it finds is not size-monotonic.

The pairwise off-diagonal correlation between per-stim V-axis
projections decomposes cleanly. \emph{Within} Qwen3 the off-diagonal
is essentially saturated ($r{=}{+}0.995 \pm 0.003$ across $15$ pairs):
all six sizes find the same direction. \emph{Across} families
Qwen3$\leftrightarrow$non-Qwen drops to $r{=}{+}0.585 \pm 0.289$ over
$48$ pairs, with Mistral-7B as the closest non-Qwen neighbour
(Qwen3$\leftrightarrow$Mistral mean $r{=}{+}0.939$). Convergence is
therefore tight within a modern LLM family and looser across
families---consistent with a ``modern-LLM manifold'' interpretation
rather than a strict universality claim.
The full per-LLM table is in Appendix~\ref{app:per-llm-deep-dive}.

A single Qwen3-4B run gives this axis surprising external validity.
Projecting onto it recovers SST-2 sentiment at zero-shot AUC
$0.832$~\citep{socher2013recursive}---matching a supervised
logistic-regression baseline trained on $5$k in-domain examples
($0.837$)---and pushes Yelp Polarity to $0.938$~\citep{yelp2015}; the
full sentiment table (IMDB, TweetEval, Rotten Tomatoes) and the two
word-level lexicon recoveries (Hu \& Liu, Warriner) are in
Appendix~\ref{app:per-llm-deep-dive}.

The V-axis is a late-layer phenomenon: the cosine between the
input-layer and the penultimate-layer V-axis stays below $0.30$
across Qwen3 sizes. The same V-shape---axes that converge only in
the last few layers---appears across five LM families and across
BERT, RoBERTa, and DeBERTa, peaking at $L_{\max}{-}1$ and ruling out
tokeniser or training-objective artefacts. Gemma-4 is the lone
outlier, with a text-side V-axis that peaks at $L{=}1$ but brain
alignment that survives at mid-layers (\S\ref{sec:vaxis-brain}).

The recipe is also concept-generic, cross-lingual, and cross-modal.
Beyond valence, $20$ of $20$ further concepts we tried in the same
form produce a working axis and $17$ exceed AUC $0.95$
(Appendix~\ref{app:concept-library}). On mT0-base
\citep{muennighoff2022mt0} the recipe exceeds the English ceiling on
Japanese, Arabic, and Russian at $5\times$ smaller
scale~(Appendix~\ref{app:multilingual-vision}). A CLIP-text V-axis
ranks OASIS~\citep{kurdi2017oasis,radford2021clip} valence at
$r{=}0.869$ and arousal at $r{=}0.803$, both beating supervised ridge.
Specificity controls bound the recipe: a nonce-word ablation drops
SST-2 to chance, random-Gaussian directions give chance lexicon
recovery, and arousal transfers in vision but stays below detection
in text---a clean modality split documented in
Appendix~\ref{app:specificity-controls}.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

A single direction extracted from nine LLM stories, the cohort
response of $123$ subjects watching emotional videos, and the
penultimate features of a classifier trained on those videos converge
on the same one-dimensional axis, without any of them seeing the
others' data---consistent with the Platonic Representation
Hypothesis~\citep{huh2024platonic} extended to the
LLM$\leftrightarrow$brain pair. The cohort signal is carried by an
\emph{anger-versus-warm-positive} contrast on $9$ of the $28$ stimuli,
and every brain finding replicates on SEED-V; the convergence is not
a peculiarity of one recording.

The negative half is the part with consequences. Adding the V-axis
back as an auxiliary loss perturbs the saturated component while
leaving the load-bearing residual untouched; twenty-five interventions
reduce to one principle, with the transition at FACED-9 BACC
$[0.62, 0.66]$ (dataset-specific magnitude; principle generalises
to KD/RSA/contrastive). The corresponding positive prescription,
ensemble gain as variance reduction in the orthogonal complement,
is exactly what our $0.6948$/$0.6755$ SOTA
measures~(Appendix~\ref{app:discussion-extras}).

\section{Brain Topography of the Valence Axis}
\label{sec:topography}

Region-mean $|r|$ at every (channel, band) pair on FACED is
$0.212$ in occipital channels (Oz, O1, O2, POZ, PO3, PO4), $0.181$
in parietal, $0.180$ in central, and $0.162$ in frontal (FP1, FP2,
FPZ, F3, F4, FZ, F7, F8). The strongest single cells are PO3/$\gamma$
($r{=}{+}0.48$), F7/$\beta$ ($-0.47$), and O1/$\gamma$ ($+0.44$);
the dominant spatial gradient runs posterior-to-anterior, so the
V-axis is a predominantly visual-cortex phenomenon for video-evoked
emotion (Figure~\ref{fig:topomap-5band}). Right-minus-left frontal-alpha
asymmetry remains positive in our data (F4-F3 $+0.0062$, F8-F7
$+0.0155$, Fp2-Fp1 $+0.0116$), consistent with Davidson's predicted
direction~\citep{davidson1992anterior,coan2004frontal}, with the
largest single frontal asymmetry at Fp2-Fp1/$\delta$ ($+0.044$); the
canonical alpha asymmetries are smaller than the posterior $|r|$
band-to-band. This is a paradigm-level finding for video-evoked
emotion rather than a refutation of FAA, which was developed for
static-stimulus paradigms; within-subject FAA effects are a separate
question this cohort-level analysis does not address.

The cohort signal is not a smooth gradient but an
\emph{anger-versus-warm-positive} contrast carried by nine clips:
cohort $r{=}{+}0.870$ at PO3/$\gamma$ for Anger, Amusement, and
Tenderness, against $r{=}{-}0.015$ for the remaining $19$ mid-valence
stimuli. Removing Anger alone shrinks the cohort correlation to
$+0.327$, and an anger-weighted target $\{5,3,3,1\}$ lifts the V-axis
ceiling from $r{=}0.478$ to $r{=}0.714$---the strongest possible V-axis
supervision target we test in \S\ref{sec:saturation}. The
between-subject cohort effect coexists with a Simpson's paradox at
the per-subject level (cohort $r{=}0.48$ vs.\ per-subject $-0.062$,
oracle $|r|{=}0.616$~\citep{sani2024crosssubject}); the time-resolved
peaks coincide with classical EEG markers in the LPP
window~\citep{hajcak2010ofthelpp,codispoti2023arousal,muller1999gamma};
theta-gamma phase-amplitude coupling does not mediate the
V-axis~\citep{canolty2010cfc}. Full caveats and time-resolved analysis
are in Appendix~\ref{app:topography-deep-dive}.

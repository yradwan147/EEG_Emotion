\section{A Universal Valence Axis in Language Models}
\label{sec:vaxis-llms}

\paragraph{Extraction protocol.}
Given a language model with hidden dimension $D$ and the FACED 9-emotion
taxonomy, we author one short emotion-evocative story per class, generate
$n=50$ paraphrases per story by independent LLM calls, and take the mean
last-token hidden state at the penultimate layer $L_{\max}{-}1$ to
obtain nine class centroids $c_1,\dots,c_9 \in \mathbb{R}^D$. Mean-centred
PCA on the $9 \times D$ matrix gives the \textbf{V-axis} as the unit
vector along $\mathrm{PC}_1$, oriented so that
$\langle c_{\mathrm{Joy}}, v\rangle > 0$. We use Qwen3-4B at the
penultimate layer ($L_{\max}-1=35$ of 36) as the lead model unless
otherwise stated; the recipe is robust to paraphrase budget (zero-shot
SST-2 AUC $0.74$ at $n{=}1$, rising to $0.832$ at our canonical
setting), and prompt-rephrasing robustness gives mean cross-prompt
$r > 0.93$ across the LLMs we test (full protocol, paraphrase quality
controls in Appendix~\ref{app:vaxis-protocol}).

\paragraph{Universality across 14 LLMs.}
We extract the V-axis from 14 language models spanning three orders of
magnitude (Qwen3 at all six dense sizes 0.6B--32B, Llama-4-Scout-17B,
Mistral-7B, Phi-2-2.7B, Pythia-1.4B, BLOOM-560M, TinyLlama-1.1B,
Gemma-27B, Gemma-4-31B). The mean off-diagonal Pearson correlation
between per-stim V-axis projections is $0.65$. Three regimes emerge
against a behavioural valence reference: a top tier ($n{=}7$, per-stim
$r>0.85$): all six Qwen3 sizes plus Mistral-7B, mean $r_{\mathrm{behav}}=0.939$;
a middle tier ($n{=}4$, $0.6 \le r \le 0.85$): Llama-4-Scout, Gemma-27B,
Gemma-4-31B, Phi-2; an out-tier ($n{=}3$, $r<0.4$): Pythia-1.4B,
TinyLlama-1.1B, BLOOM-560M. Within-Qwen3 scaling is essentially flat
($r=+0.568$, $p=0.24$, $n=6$): universality is \emph{not} a
size-monotonic phenomenon (full per-LLM table in
Appendix~\ref{app:per-llm-deep-dive}).

\paragraph{External validity on sentiment.}
Zero-shot AUCs at the V-axis projection (Qwen3-4B): SST-2 binary
$0.832$ \citep{socher2013recursive} (matching a supervised LR baseline
trained on 5k in-domain samples at $0.837$), IMDB $0.857$
\citep{maas2011imdb}, Yelp Polarity $0.938$ \citep{yelp2015}, Twitter
SemEval-2017 $0.927$ \citep{rosenthal2017semeval}, Rotten Tomatoes
$0.799$ \citep{pang2005seeing}. The V-axis also recovers Hu \& Liu
opinion polarity \citep{hu2004mining} at $|r|=0.731$ ($n{=}1500$
sampled words) and Warriner et al.\ valence
\citep{warriner2013norms} at $|r|=0.703$ ($n{=}3000$).

\paragraph{V-shape: a late-layer phenomenon.}
The V-axis is newly computed in late transformer layers, not inherited
from input embeddings: cosine between input-layer and final-layer
V-axis is small (under $0.30$ across Qwen3 sizes) with a sharp emergence
in the last few layers. The same V-shape emerges across five LM families
and across
BERT, RoBERTa, DeBERTa, peaking at $L_{\max}{-}1$ --- ruling out
tokeniser or training-objective artefacts. Gemma-4 is the lone outlier
(text V-axis peaks at $L{=}1$); brain alignment survives at mid-layers
(Section~\ref{sec:vaxis-brain}).

\paragraph{Generality, controls, and scope.}
The recipe is concept-generic (20/20 working axes, 17/20 at AUC
$\ge0.95$, $20\!\times\!20$ transfer-matrix gap $+0.13$;
Appendix~\ref{app:concept-library}), cross-lingual (mT0-base
\citep{muennighoff2022mt0} on Japanese/Arabic/Russian exceeds the
English ceiling at $5\!\times$ smaller scale;
Appendix~\ref{app:multilingual-vision}), and cross-modal (CLIP V-axis
ranks OASIS \citep{kurdi2017oasis,radford2021clip} valence at
$r=0.869$, arousal at $r=0.803$, both beating supervised ridge). Three
specificity controls (Appendix~\ref{app:specificity-controls}) bound
the recipe: nonce-word ablation drops SST-2 AUC to chance ($\approx
0.52$, signal is semantic, not syntactic); random-Gaussian matched
directions give SST-2 AUC $\approx 0.51$ and lexicon $|r|\approx 0.10$;
arousal
transfers in vision ($r=0.803$) and is below detection threshold in
text (NRC arousal recovery $\le 13\%$ vs.\ valence $\ge 76\%$),
delineating the scope of the recipe.

\section{EEG Models Converge to the LLM V-axis Without Being Asked}
\label{sec:convergence}

We collect $36$ EEG-emotion checkpoints spanning two architectures
(CBraMod~\citep{wang2025cbramod} and EMOD~\citep{chen2025emod}),
three training regimes, and six recipe variants. For each checkpoint
we extract balanced test accuracy and two V-axis encoding scores
from the $28$-stimulus penultimate features: a class-PC1 alignment
$|\mathrm{corr}(\mathrm{PC}_1(\{\mu_k\}), v_{\mathrm{CLIP}})|$ across
the nine class centroids, and a within-class residual alignment
between the validation-selected best residual PC and the $28$-stim
CLIP V-axis residual. Across $n{=}36$ checkpoints
$\mathrm{Pearson}(\mathrm{BACC}, \mathrm{class\text{-}PC1\text{-}r}){=}{+}0.885$
and $\mathrm{Pearson}(\mathrm{BACC}, \mathrm{within\text{-}resid\text{-}r}){=}{+}0.738$
(Figure~\ref{fig:crossarch}). A $1000$-direction random Gaussian null
matched in $L_2$ norm to $v_{\mathrm{CLIP}}$ places class-PC1 at the
$93.5^{\mathrm{th}}$ percentile ($p_{\mathrm{one}}{=}0.066$); the
9-class PC1 subspace is intrinsically low-dimensional ($\sigma{=}0.62$),
so we foreground the within-class residual---operating in
$D_{\mathrm{resid}}\approx D-9$---as the statistically robust signal.
The class-PC1 basin itself is saturated across seeds
($|r|\in[0.60, 0.77]$, mean $0.69 \pm 0.05$;
Appendix~\ref{app:convergence-deep-dive}).

The within-class residual is operationally important because it
predicts ensemble contribution. Across the $10$ vanilla $d{=}6$,
$f{=}128$ checkpoints used to build our SOTA ensemble, leave-one-out
contributions
$\Delta_k = \mathrm{BACC}(\mathrm{ens}) - \mathrm{BACC}(\mathrm{ens}\setminus k)$
correlate with within-residual $|r|$ at $\mathbf{+0.743}$ ($p{=}0.014$,
$n{=}10$); a top-$7$ ensemble reaches $\mathbf{0.6962}$ BACC versus a
bottom-$7$ ensemble at $\mathbf{0.6829}$, a $+0.0133$ gap above a
random-$7$ baseline ($z{=}{+}2.0$).

\begin{quote}
\textbf{Residual Contribution Law.} \emph{For a saturated EEG
classifier with within-class residual encoding of the V-axis at
$|r|=\rho_k$, that checkpoint's leave-one-out contribution to a
softmax-averaged ensemble scales linearly with $\rho_k$, with slope
$\hat\beta = +0.74$ ($p{=}0.014$, $n{=}10$, FACED 9-class).}
\end{quote}

The Law is correlational; an Arditi-style causal
intervention~\citep{arditi2024refusal} closes the loop. At inference
time on each of the $10$ SOTA-pool checkpoints we project penultimate
features onto the orthogonal complement of $v_{\mathrm{resid}}$
without retraining. Residual ablation produces
$\Delta\mathrm{BACC}\in[-0.024, -0.006]$ (mean $-0.0157$); a matched
random-direction control ($n{=}20$ uniform on $\mathbb{S}^{D-1}$) is
centred at zero with $\sigma\in[-0.0006, +0.0032]$. Every checkpoint
clears the null parametrically at $p<0.001$ (one-sided, mean
$z\approx 7.7$), confirming that V-axis residual encoding is
load-bearing across the pool. External V-axis loss therefore
optimises the \emph{saturated} class-mean component while perturbing
the \emph{useful} within-class residual---the prediction we test
directly via $25$ interventions in \S\ref{sec:saturation}.

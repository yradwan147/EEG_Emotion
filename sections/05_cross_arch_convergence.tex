\section{EEG Models Converge to the LLM V-axis Without Being Asked}
\label{sec:convergence}

We collect 36 EEG-emotion checkpoints spanning two architectures
(CBraMod \citep{wang2025cbramod}, EMOD \citep{chen2025emod}), three
training regimes, and six recipe variants. For each checkpoint $m$ we
extract balanced test accuracy and two V-axis encoding scores from the
28-stimulus penultimate features $H(m)$: \textbf{class-PC1 alignment}
$|\mathrm{corr}(\mathrm{PC}_1(\{\mu_k\}), v_{\mathrm{CLIP}})|$ across the
9 class centroids $\mu_k$, and \textbf{within-class residual} alignment
between the validation-selected best residual PC and the 28-stim CLIP
V-axis residual.

\paragraph{Both metrics correlate with accuracy.}
Figure~\ref{fig:crossarch} summarises the three-panel result.
Across $n{=}36$ checkpoints,
$\mathrm{Pearson}(\mathrm{BACC}, \mathrm{class\text{-}PC1\text{-}r}) = +0.885$
and $\mathrm{Pearson}(\mathrm{BACC}, \mathrm{within\text{-}resid\text{-}r}) = +0.738$.
A 1000-direction random Gaussian null matched in $L_2$ norm to
$v_{\mathrm{CLIP}}$ places class-PC1 at the $93.5^{\mathrm{th}}$
percentile ($p_{\mathrm{one}}=0.066$); the 9-class PC1 subspace is
intrinsically low-dimensional ($\sigma=0.62$). We therefore foreground
the within-class residual --- operating in $D_{\mathrm{resid}} \approx
D-9$ --- as the statistically robust signal. Per-arch breakdown, full null,
and class-PC1 basin saturation ($|r|\in[0.60, 0.77]$ across 10 seeds,
mean $0.69 \pm 0.05$) in Appendix~\ref{app:convergence-deep-dive}.

\paragraph{Within-class residual predicts ensemble contribution.}
Across the 10 vanilla $d{=}6$, $f{=}128$ checkpoints used to build our
SOTA ensemble, leave-one-out contributions
$\Delta_k = \mathrm{BACC}(\mathrm{ens}) - \mathrm{BACC}(\mathrm{ens}\setminus k)$
correlate with within-residual $|r|$ at \textbf{$+0.743$} ($p=0.014$,
$n=10$). Top-7 ensemble BACC: $\mathbf{0.6962}$; bottom-7: $\mathbf{0.6829}$;
$\Delta=+0.0133$ vs.\ random-7 baseline ($z=+2.0$).

\begin{quote}
\textbf{Residual Contribution Law.} \emph{For a saturated EEG classifier
with within-class residual encoding of the V-axis at $|r| = \rho_k$,
that checkpoint's leave-one-out contribution to a softmax-averaged
ensemble scales linearly with $\rho_k$, with slope $\hat\beta = +0.74$
($p = 0.014$, $n = 10$, FACED 9-class).}
\end{quote}

\paragraph{Causal closure.}
The Residual Contribution Law is correlational. An Arditi-style causal
intervention \citep{arditi2024refusal} closes the loop: at inference
time on each of the 10 SOTA-pool checkpoints, we project penultimate
features onto the orthogonal complement of $v_{\mathrm{resid}}$ (no
retraining). V-axis residual ablation produces $\Delta\mathrm{BACC}\in
[-0.024, -0.006]$ (mean $-0.0157$); a matched random-direction control
($n=20$ uniform on $\mathbb{S}^{D-1}$) is centred at $0$ with $\sigma
\in [-0.0006, +0.0032]$. Every checkpoint clears the null parametrically
at $p<0.001$ (one-sided, mean $z\approx 7.7$); the population-mean drop
$\overline{\Delta\mathrm{BACC}}=-0.0157$ confirms that V-axis residual
encoding is load-bearing across the pool
(Appendix~\ref{app:convergence-deep-dive}, Figure~\ref{fig:lf16-causal}).

External V-axis loss therefore optimises the \emph{saturated}
class-mean component while perturbing the \emph{useful} within-class
residual --- the prediction tested directly by the 25 interventions of
Section~\ref{sec:saturation}.

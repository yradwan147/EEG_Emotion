\section{Replication on a Second EEG Dataset (SEED-V)}
\label{sec:vaxis-brain-seedv}

A second EEG dataset, SEED-V~\citep{liu2022seedv}, lets us see whether
the same picture emerges when nothing is shared with FACED. We
re-build the V-axis from scratch on its five emotion classes on $16$
subjects and $62$ channels, and we re-test every FACED claim with no
parameter shared between datasets. The cohort EEG--LLM signal returns
at $r{=}{+}0.6159$ ($p<10^{-5}$) at the P1/$\theta$ cell, the same
posterior-dominant gradient appears, and adding the V-axis as
supervision again moves accuracy by a margin within seed noise. The
within-Qwen3 universality story re-emerges as well: the broader
$14$-LLM forest projects onto SEED-V EEG with the same three-tier
structure we see on FACED.

Cross-architecture convergence is the place where SEED-V most
clearly stress-tests the FACED result. Across $15$ stock CBraMod
SEED-V checkpoints, accuracy correlates with V-axis encoding strength
at $r{=}{+}0.601$ ($p{=}0.018$)---a correlation built on a different
EEG dataset, a different label space, and a different best cell, yet
with the same sign and magnitude as FACED. The causal closure
replicates harder still: ablating the V-axis residual direction at
inference drops accuracy on every one of the $15$ checkpoints
($\Delta\mathrm{BACC} \in [-0.029, -0.009]$, mean $-0.0173$) at mean
$z{=}{+}23.6$ above a matched random-direction null---roughly three
times the FACED effect, with no checkpoint surviving the null at
$p<0.001$. Full Step 1--5 numerics, FAA bars, and per-checkpoint
details are in Appendix~\ref{app:seedv-full}.

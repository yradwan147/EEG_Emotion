\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

A line of work from Toneva and Wehbe~\citep{toneva2019interpreting},
Schrimpf et~al.~\citep{schrimpf2021neural}, Goldstein et~al.~\citep{goldstein2022shared},
and Caucheteux and King~\citep{caucheteux2022brains} established that
representations from transformer language models linearly predict
human brain responses---across fMRI, ECoG, and MEG---to natural
language. The emerging picture, formalised by Huh et~al.\ as the
\emph{Platonic Representation Hypothesis}~\citep{huh2024platonic}, is
that representations across modalities and architectures converge to
a shared statistical model of reality. We ask whether this story
extends from language to \emph{emotion}, and whether convergence runs
both ways: not only do LLM directions predict brain responses, but
classifiers trained on those brain signals find the same direction in
return. Our subject is the \emph{valence axis}, the principal
direction along which human affect is organised
\citep{russell1980circumplex,warriner2013norms}.

We define a one-dimensional \emph{V-axis} extracted from nine short
emotion-evocative LLM prompts, one per FACED class, by taking
mean-centred PCA on their late-layer class centroids. Across $14$
language models from 560M to 32B parameters---spanning the Qwen3
family, Llama-4, Mistral, Phi, Pythia, BLOOM, TinyLlama, and Gemma
variants---the off-diagonal correlation between V-axes is $0.65$, and
within the Qwen3 family the axis is essentially scale-invariant. A
single Qwen3-4B run recovers SST-2 sentiment at zero-shot AUC
$0.832$~\citep{socher2013recursive} and Yelp Polarity at $0.938$
\citep{yelp2015}, with the full benchmark and lexicon table in
\S\ref{sec:vaxis-llms}.

The same axis projects onto FACED EEG~\citep{chen2023faced} ($123$
subjects watching $28$ emotional video clips) at cohort
$r{=}0.87$ ($p<10^{-9}$). Critically, the signal lives in posterior
visual cortex---not the frontal regions long implicated by Davidson's
frontal-alpha asymmetry hypothesis
\citep{davidson1992anterior,coan2004frontal}, which we replicate in
direction at smaller magnitude.

The same axis emerges \emph{spontaneously} in EEG classifiers trained
on the 9-class label without any V-axis supervision. Across $36$
checkpoints from two architectures
(CBraMod~\citep{wang2025cbramod}, EMOD~\citep{chen2025emod}), accuracy
correlates with V-axis encoding strength at $r{=}{+}0.885$ in the
class-PC1 subspace and $r{=}{+}0.738$ in the within-class residual.
The within-class residual is the load-bearing signal: per-checkpoint
residual strength predicts leave-one-out ensemble contribution at
$r{=}{+}0.74$ ($p{=}0.014$, $n{=}10$), and an Arditi-style causal
ablation~\citep{arditi2024refusal} of the residual direction drops
balanced accuracy on every checkpoint at mean $z{\approx}7.7\sigma$
above a matched random-direction null. LLMs and EEG classifiers
arrive at the same axis without sharing data, completing a three-way
LLM--brain--classifier triangle and giving direct cross-modal
evidence for the Platonic Representation Hypothesis at biological
scale.

What happens when we add this universal axis as auxiliary supervision?
We test $25$ representation-alignment interventions drawn from seven
years of literature---auxiliary regression, knowledge distillation,
RSA, contrastive losses, PEFT adapters, curriculum schedules,
multi-LLM ensembles, channel-targeted topographic losses, and
anger-weighted variants matched to the analytical valence ceiling. None
help our strong recipe; $16$ produce statistically significant
accuracy decrements ($\Delta\text{BACC}{\le}{-}0.013$, $p{<}0.05$) and
five intervention families exhibit \emph{monotonic} destruction with
intervention strength. The transition from neutral to harmful is
sharp, sits at FACED-9 BACC in $[0.62, 0.66]$, and disappears at weak
baselines. We call this regularity \emph{saturation}: above an
empirically located threshold, the V-axis is the latent the network
naturally finds, and additional supervision is redundant gradient
noise. The two-tier picture this creates---a saturated class-mean
basin shared across seeds, plus a seed-specific within-class residual
that is the only subspace where averaging recovers signal---directly
yields a new FACED 9-class state of the art: a uniform softmax
ensemble across $5$ checkpoints at $e{=}100$ and $5$ at $e{=}150$
(both $d{=}6$, $f{=}128$) reaches $\mathbf{0.6948}$ BACC, $+21.5\%$
relative over CBraMod's $0.572$ and $+10.6\%$ over EmotionKD's
$0.628$~\citep{liu2023emotionkd}, with the best val-selected single
checkpoint at $\mathbf{0.6755}$. All five claims replicate on
SEED-V~\citep{liu2022seedv}, where the V-axis re-built from scratch
on five classes recovers cohort EEG at $r{=}{+}0.6159$, posterior
dominance, cross-architecture convergence at $r{=}{+}0.601$,
saturation, and causal closure at mean $z{=}{+}23.6$ across $15$
checkpoints.

\paragraph{Contributions.} We make five contributions. \textbf{(1)} A
one-dimensional V-axis extracted from nine LLM emotion stories that
predicts language sentiment, vision affect, and cohort EEG response
across two datasets, generalising across $14$ LLMs
(\S\ref{sec:vaxis-llms}--\S\ref{sec:vaxis-brain-seedv}).
\textbf{(2)} Spontaneous cross-architecture convergence: $36$ EEG
checkpoints encode the same axis without supervision, with a
\emph{Residual Contribution Law} linking per-checkpoint residual
strength to ensemble contribution and a causal Arditi ablation
closing the loop (\S\ref{sec:convergence}).
\textbf{(3)} A posterior-dominant brain topography distinguishing
video-evoked emotion from the static-stimulus regime in which
frontal-alpha asymmetry was developed (\S\ref{sec:topography}).
\textbf{(4)} The \emph{Saturation Regularity}: $25$ interventions
unified under one principle, with the transition empirically located
at FACED-9 BACC $[0.62, 0.66]$ (\S\ref{sec:saturation}).
\textbf{(5)} A new FACED 9-class state of the art at $\mathbf{0.6948}$
ensemble and $\mathbf{0.6755}$ single, with the gain mechanistically
tied to the within-class residual (\S\ref{sec:sota}).

\paragraph{What this result is not.} Saturation is an empirical
regularity rather than an analytical theorem; the brain picture is
cohort-averaged across 123 subjects, with within-subject heterogeneity
documented as a Simpson's-paradox caveat (\S\ref{sec:topography}); and
the threshold $[0.62, 0.66]$ is FACED-specific in magnitude. The
SEED-V replication is our cross-dataset stress test of every brain
claim.

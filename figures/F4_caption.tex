\textbf{Per-channel V-axis encoding peaks in posterior cortex, not the frontal asymmetry sites of classical FAA.}
Five MNE-style scalp topomaps, one per band ($\delta$, $\theta$, $\alpha$, $\beta$, $\gamma$), of the cohort Pearson correlation between the CLIP V-axis (28 stim projections) and per-channel band power on FACED ($n{=}123$ subjects, 30 channels of the 10--20 layout; A1/A2 mastoids excluded from plotting).
Color: \textcolor[HTML]{1f77b4}{blue} = positive correlation, \textcolor[HTML]{d62728}{red} = negative.
Yellow circles mark the top channels by $|r|$ (PO3, F7, O1).
Region-mean $|r|$ ranks occipital ($0.21$) $>$ parietal ($0.18$) $>$ central ($0.18$) $>$ frontal ($0.16$); the peak cell is PO3 / $\gamma$, $r{=}+0.48$.
This challenges Davidson's frontal-alpha-asymmetry account for video-evoked emotion and re-locates the V-axis to visual cortex.

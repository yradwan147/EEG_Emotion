\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

A line of work from Toneva and Wehbe \citep{toneva2019interpreting},
Schrimpf et~al.\ \citep{schrimpf2021neural}, Goldstein et~al.\
\citep{goldstein2022shared}, and Caucheteux and King
\citep{caucheteux2022brains} established that representations from
transformer LMs linearly predict human brain responses --- in fMRI,
ECoG, and MEG --- to natural language. The emerging picture, formalised
by Huh et~al.\ as the \emph{Platonic Representation Hypothesis}
\citep{huh2024platonic}, is that representations across modalities and
architectures converge to a shared statistical model of reality. We ask
whether this story extends from \emph{language} to \emph{emotion}, and
back from biological neural data to artificial networks trained on
biological signals. Specifically, we study the \emph{valence axis} ---
the principal direction along which human emotion is organised
\citep{russell1980circumplex,warriner2013norms}.

\paragraph{The valence axis as a universal latent.}
Figure~\ref{fig:universal-vaxis} previews the three panels we will defend.
We define a one-dimensional \emph{V-axis} extracted from nine short LLM
prompts (one per FACED emotion class). PCA on the resulting nine class
centroids yields a single direction. Across 14 LLMs from 560M to 32B parameters the mean off-diagonal
Pearson correlation between V-axes is $0.65$. Zero-shot V-axis
projection from a single Qwen3-4B run recovers SST-2 sentiment at
$\mathrm{AUC}=0.832$ \citep{socher2013recursive}, Yelp Polarity at
$\mathrm{AUC}=0.938$ \citep{yelp2015}, and Hu \& Liu opinion polarity
at $|r|=0.731$ \citep{hu2004mining}
(Section~\ref{sec:vaxis-llms}).

\paragraph{The V-axis is in the brain.}
The V-axis predicts cohort-averaged FACED EEG \citep{chen2023faced} (123
subjects, 28 emotional videos, 32 channels at 250\,Hz) at $r=0.87$
($p<10^{-9}$, $n=28$ stimuli). Crucially, the axis lives in
\emph{posterior visual cortex}
(occipital $|r|=0.21$, parietal $0.18$), not in frontal regions long
implicated by Davidson's frontal-alpha asymmetry hypothesis
\citep{davidson1992anterior,coan2004frontal} which we replicate in
direction at smaller magnitude. The cohort signal is dominated by a
9-stimulus subset (anger, amusement, tenderness; cohort $r{=}0.870$ vs.\
$r{=}{-}0.015$ on the remaining 19 mid-valence stimuli)
(Section~\ref{sec:topography}).

\paragraph{EEG models converge to the V-axis without being asked.}
Across 36 checkpoints from two architectures (CBraMod
\citep{wang2025cbramod}, EMOD \citep{chen2025emod}), accuracy correlates
with V-axis encoding strength at $r=+0.885$ in class-PC1 and $r=+0.738$
in the within-class residual --- without any explicit alignment
supervision. The within-class residual is the statistically robust
signal: per-checkpoint residual $|r|$ predicts leave-one-out ensemble
contribution at $r=+0.74$ ($p=0.014$, $n=10$), and a causal
Arditi-style ablation \citep{arditi2024refusal} of the residual
direction drops BACC well above a matched random-direction null on
every checkpoint (mean $z\approx 7.7$, all $p<0.001$;
Section~\ref{sec:convergence}).

\paragraph{The saturation regularity: 25 alignment interventions, one principle.}
What happens when we add the V-axis as auxiliary supervision? We test
25 representation-alignment interventions --- auxiliary regression, KD
\citep{hinton2015distilling}, label smoothing, soft SupCon
\citep{khosla2020supcon}, RSA \citep{kriegeskorte2008rsa}, InfoNCE
\citep{vandenoord2018cpc}, two-stage pretrain--finetune, multi-LLM
ensembling, channel-targeted topographic losses, Procrustes,
PEFT \citep{hu2022lora,liu2022ia3}, curriculum schedules, and class-weighted
variants matched to the analytical 9-stim ceiling. None help our strong
recipe; 16 \emph{significantly hurt} it (e.g.\ frontal-mask
$\lambda{=}0.5$: $\Delta=-0.052$, $p=0.0015$; anger-weighted
$\lambda{=}0.5$: $\Delta=-0.054$, $p=0.0003$). The same interventions
are within seed noise of zero on weak baselines ($p>0.2$ throughout).
This is one operational principle: the V-axis is the latent these
models naturally find; once arrived, additional supervision is
redundant gradient noise (Section~\ref{sec:saturation}).

\paragraph{Principled ensembling around the universal latent.}
Each EEG checkpoint encodes an essentially identical class-mean V-axis
basin (inter-seed class-PC1 $|r|$ range $0.60$--$0.77$, saturated) but
imperfectly approximates the \emph{within-class} residual --- the only
subspace where averaging recovers signal. Top-7 by within-residual $|r|$
ensemble to $0.6962$ vs.\ bottom-7 to $0.6829$ ($z=+2.0$). Uniform
softmax averaging across 5\,e100 + 5\,e150 $d{=}6$ checkpoints achieves
\textbf{0.6948 BACC}, $+21.5\%$ relative over CBraMod's $0.572$ and
$+10.6\%$ over EmotionKD's $0.628$ \citep{liu2023emotionkd}. A single
val-selected checkpoint reaches \textbf{0.6755}, $+2.6\%$ over the
published EMOD single-seed baseline of $0.6581$.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
We deliver: \textbf{(1) Cross-modal universality}
(\S\ref{sec:vaxis-llms}--\S\ref{sec:vaxis-brain-seedv}): one V-axis
predicting language sentiment, vision affect, and EEG cohort response
(FACED $r{=}0.87$, SEED-V $r{=}0.62$). \textbf{(2) Spontaneous
convergence} (\S\ref{sec:convergence}): $r{=}+0.885$ class-PC1,
$r{=}+0.738$ residual across 36 checkpoints; $r{=}+0.74$ ($p{=}0.014$)
ensemble-contribution prediction; causal closure via residual ablation.
\textbf{(3) Brain topography} (\S\ref{sec:topography}): posterior visual
cortex dominance; 9-stim emotional-pole contrast. \textbf{(4) Saturation
regularity} (\S\ref{sec:saturation}): 25-intervention unification;
threshold in BACC interval $[0.62, 0.66]$. \textbf{(5) New FACED 9-class
SOTA} (\S\ref{sec:sota}): $\mathbf{0.6948}$ ensemble, $\mathbf{0.6755}$
single, with mechanistic explanation tying gain to within-class V-axis
residual.

\paragraph{What this result is not.}
Saturation is empirical, not a theorem; the brain picture is
cohort-averaged across 123 subjects (within-subject heterogeneity
documented as a Simpson's-paradox caveat, \S\ref{sec:topography}); the
threshold is FACED-specific, with cross-dataset replication on SEED-V
(\S\ref{sec:vaxis-brain-seedv}).

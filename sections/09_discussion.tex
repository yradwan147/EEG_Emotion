\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

A single direction extracted from nine LLM stories, the cohort
response of $123$ subjects watching emotional videos, and the
penultimate features of a classifier trained on those videos converge
on the same one-dimensional axis, without any of them seeing the
others' data. This is consistent with the Platonic Representation
Hypothesis~\citep{huh2024platonic} extended to the
LLM$\leftrightarrow$brain pair, in both directions: LLM-derived
directions predict brain responses, and brain-trained classifiers
find the LLM-side direction in return. The cohort EEG correlation
($r{=}0.87$ at PO3/$\gamma$) is carried by an
\emph{anger-versus-warm-positive} contrast on $9$ of the $28$
stimuli---a scope we report transparently in \S\ref{sec:topography}.
Every brain finding replicates on SEED-V, most sharply through a
directional ablation whose normalised effect is $\sim$1.6$\times$
larger than on FACED in $\Delta/\text{baseline}$ terms ($3.9\%$ vs.\
$2.4\%$); the convergence is not a peculiarity of one recording.

The negative half of the story is the part with consequences. Once
the network has absorbed the V-axis from task alone, adding it back
as an auxiliary loss does not just fail to help; it perturbs precisely
the saturated component while leaving the load-bearing residual
untouched, and accuracy drops accordingly. Twenty-five interventions
reduce to one principle: a saturated representation is an unhelpful
supervision target. We locate the empirical transition at FACED-9
BACC $[0.62, 0.66]$; the principle should hold elsewhere but the
threshold magnitude is dataset- and architecture-specific. The same observation gives a positive
prescription: ensemble gain on a saturated network is variance
reduction in the orthogonal complement of the converged
subspace---generalising bias--variance \citep{geman1992neural} and
linear-mode connectivity \citep{frankle2020linearmode}---and that is
exactly what our $0.6948$/$0.6755$ FACED state of the art measures.

The regularity should generalise: modern recipes for concept
supervision (KD, RSA, contrastive auxiliary losses, soft-prompt
projections) all align penultimate features with a chosen geometry,
and that gain has a horizon---it shrinks as task supervision finds
the same geometry on its own. Per-subject residual adaptation, which
lifts the within-class signal without disturbing the basin, is the
immediate next step (Appendix~\ref{app:discussion-extras},~\ref{app:negatives}).

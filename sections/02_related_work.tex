\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Brain--LM alignment.}
Toneva and Wehbe~\citep{toneva2019interpreting} first showed that
mid-layer transformer activations predict fMRI responses to narrative
text; Schrimpf et~al.~\citep{schrimpf2021neural} ($43$ LMs against
fMRI/ECoG), Goldstein et~al.~\citep{goldstein2022shared}
(intracranial), Caucheteux and King~\citep{caucheteux2022brains}
(MEG), and Padakanti and Oota~\citep{padakanti2025meg} (per-sensor
topographic maps) extended the picture across modalities. We differ
by working at full $123$-subject EEG scale, by studying \emph{emotion}
rather than language, and by showing alignment in both directions:
LLM-derived directions predict brain responses, and EEG classifiers
trained on those brain signals converge to the same direction.

\paragraph{Concept directions in LLMs.}
Arditi et~al.~\citep{arditi2024refusal} showed that refusal in
instruction-tuned LMs is mediated by a single direction in
residual-stream activations; ablating it eliminates refusal, adding it
induces it. Earlier work in this family includes
\citep{li2023inferencetime,zou2023repe,kim2018tcav}. The V-axis is
extracted analogously---principal component of class-mean activations
across nine emotion-story prompts---and exhibits the same
cross-architecture convergence within the modern-LLM family. We extend this line by linking
the LLM-side direction to a $123$-subject biological neural system at
the cohort level and by showing that an artificial classifier trained
on those biological signals \emph{finds} the same direction,
completing a three-way LLM--brain--classifier triangle.

\paragraph{Platonic hypothesis, ensemble theory, and FAA.}
Huh et~al.~\citep{huh2024platonic} operationalised the Platonic
hypothesis through vision--language kernel alignment; the V-axis
convergence we observe is consistent with the same hypothesis at the
LLM$\leftrightarrow$brain and LLM$\leftrightarrow$EEG-classifier pairs.
The two-tier mechanism we identify---a saturated class-mean basin
plus a seed-specific within-class residual---gives a representation-level
form of bias--variance~\citep{geman1992neural,krogh1995neural} and
linear-mode connectivity~\citep{frankle2020linearmode,wortsman2022model}.
Davidson's frontal-alpha asymmetry hypothesis~\citep{davidson1992anterior,coan2004frontal,davidson2004wellbeing}
remains the dominant electrophysiological account of emotion; we
replicate it qualitatively at smaller magnitude and add a posterior
account for video paradigms. The $25$ alignment mechanisms we test are
drawn from~\citep{hinton2015distilling,tian2020crd,kriegeskorte2008rsa,park2019rkd,sundaram2024perceptual,radford2021clip,khosla2020supcon,hu2022lora,liu2022ia3}.
The FACED-9 baselines we compare against are
CBraMod $0.572$~\citep{wang2025cbramod}, EmotionKD $0.628$~\citep{liu2023emotionkd},
and EMOD $0.6287$~\citep{chen2025emod}, with REVE~\citep{elouahidi2025reve},
LaBraM~\citep{jiang2024labram}, and EEGPT~\citep{yue2024eegpt} as
foundation-model references.

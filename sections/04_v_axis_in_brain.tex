\section{The Same Axis in Human EEG}
\label{sec:vaxis-brain}

We use FACED \citep{chen2023faced}: 123 subjects, 28 emotional video
clips (3--4 stimuli per emotion class), 32 channels at 250\,Hz, 30-second
clips. We compute 5-band differential entropy (DE) per channel per second
per stimulus, mean over time, and average across subjects to obtain a
$28 \times 32 \times 5$ cohort tensor $\mathbf{F}$. The CLIP-text V-axis
yields a 28-dimensional per-stimulus projection $v$; for each
$(\text{channel}, \text{band})$ cell we compute Pearson correlations
between $v$ and the cohort EEG response.

\paragraph{Cohort EEG--LLM alignment.}
The best cell sits at PO3/$\gamma$ at $r{=}+0.478$, with stim-level
cohort $r{=}{+}0.87$ ($n=28$, parametric $p<10^{-9}$;
Figure~\ref{fig:eeg-llm-circle}). At the per-cell
level, a 200-direction random Gaussian null matched in $L_2$ norm gives
mean $|r|=0.16$; the empirical V-axis exceeds the 95th percentile of
that null. Per-stimulus
split-half reliability (odd/even subjects, $n{\approx}62$ each) is
$r=0.988$; class-level ranking ($n=9$ centroids) hits $r{=}+0.886$ vs.\
behavioural valence. Mutual-information control on the same null is
non-significant ($p_{\mathrm{MI}}=0.115$ vs.\ linear $p_r=0.020$),
confirming the relationship is essentially linear; we use ridge
regressions throughout.

\paragraph{Posterior dominance and 9-stim contrast.}
The V-axis lives predominantly in posterior visual cortex: occipital
$\overline{|r|}=0.212$, parietal $0.181$, central $0.180$, frontal
$0.162$. The cohort signal is carried by 9 of 28 stimuli (Anger +
Amusement + Tenderness; cohort $r{=}+0.870$ at PO3/$\gamma$, vs.\
$r{=}-0.015$ on the remaining 19 mid-valence stimuli; full topography
treatment in Section~\ref{sec:topography}).

\paragraph{14 LLMs predict the brain.}
Computing the V-axis from each of the 14 LLMs and projecting onto FACED
EEG (best cell, PO3/$\gamma$): the brain ranking recapitulates the
text-domain three-tier structure. Top-tier models ($n{=}7$) recover
EEG-correlated V-axes at mean $r=+0.796 \pm 0.011$ across the Qwen3
family alone, irrespective of family or scale; out-tier models without
a clean text V-axis show the same pattern on the brain side.
Within-Qwen3 scaling is flat ($r=+0.568$, $p=0.24$, $n=6$):
\emph{universality $\gg$ scale}. Brain alignment also survives the
Gemma-4 mid-layer anomaly (Gemma-4-E2B at $L{=}17$ predicts EEG at
$r=0.819$, $p \approx 10^{-7}$). Per-LLM forest plot, mid-layer
robustness, time-locked dynamics in
Appendix~\ref{app:brain-deep-dive}.

\paragraph{Cross-EEG-dataset transfer.}
The 9-class FACED V-axis projection ranks the 5 SEED-V emotion classes
\citep{liu2022seedv} at semantic-space Pearson $r{=}+0.96$ ($p=0.008$)
without any retraining. A deeper SEED-V replication
(Section~\ref{sec:vaxis-brain-seedv}) re-derives the V-axis from
scratch and re-tests all five FACED claims, including a causal closure
matching FACED's residual-ablation analogue.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Brain--LM alignment.}
Toneva and Wehbe \citep{toneva2019interpreting} first showed that
mid-layer transformer activations predict fMRI responses to narrative
text; Schrimpf et~al.\ \citep{schrimpf2021neural} (43 LMs vs.\
fMRI/ECoG), Goldstein et~al.\ \citep{goldstein2022shared}
(intracranial), Caucheteux and King \citep{caucheteux2022brains} (MEG),
and Padakanti and Oota \citep{padakanti2025meg} (per-sensor topographic
maps) extend the picture. We differ by working at full 123-subject EEG
scale, studying \emph{emotion}, and showing alignment in both
directions: LLM-derived directions predict brain responses, and EEG
classifiers converge to the same direction.

\paragraph{Concept directions in LLMs.}
Arditi et~al.\ \citep{arditi2024refusal} show that \emph{refusal} in
instruction-tuned LMs is mediated by a single direction in
residual-stream activations; ablating it eliminates refusal, adding it
induces it. Earlier work in this style includes
\citep{li2023inferencetime,zou2023repe,kim2018tcav}. Our V-axis is
extracted analogously --- principal component of class-mean activations
across nine emotion-story prompts --- and exhibits the same
architecture-spanning universality. We differ by (i) linking the
LLM-side direction to a 123-subject biological neural system at the
cohort level, and (ii) showing that an artificial classifier trained on
those biological signals \emph{finds} the same direction, completing a
three-way LLM--brain--EEG-classifier triangle.

\paragraph{Platonic Representation Hypothesis, ensemble theory, and FAA.}
Huh et~al.\ \citep{huh2024platonic} operationalise the Platonic
Hypothesis via vision--language kernel alignment; we extend it to
LLM$\leftrightarrow$brain and LLM$\leftrightarrow$EEG-classifier pairs.
Our two-tier theory --- saturated class-mean basin (Tier 1) plus
seed-specific within-class residual (Tier 2) --- gives a
representation-level form of bias--variance \citep{geman1992neural,krogh1995neural}
and linear-mode connectivity
\citep{frankle2020linearmode,wortsman2022model}. Davidson's
frontal-alpha asymmetry hypothesis
\citep{davidson1992anterior,coan2004frontal,davidson2004wellbeing}
remains the dominant electrophysiological account of emotion; we
replicate it qualitatively at smaller magnitude and add a
posterior-dominant account for video paradigms. We test 25 alignment
mechanisms drawn from
\citep{hinton2015distilling,tian2020crd,kriegeskorte2008rsa,park2019rkd,sundaram2024perceptual,radford2021clip,khosla2020supcon,hu2022lora,liu2022ia3}
and confirm: above the saturation threshold, all hurt or do nothing.
FACED-9 baselines we compare against are CBraMod $0.572$
\citep{wang2025cbramod}, EmotionKD $0.628$ \citep{liu2023emotionkd}, and
EMOD $0.6287$ \citep{chen2025emod}, with REVE \citep{elouahidi2025reve},
LaBraM \citep{jiang2024labram}, and EEGPT \citep{yue2024eegpt} as
foundation-model references.

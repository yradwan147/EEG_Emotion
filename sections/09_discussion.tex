\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

The picture we have drawn is a triangle: a single direction extracted
from nine LLM stories, the cohort response of a hundred-plus subjects
watching emotional videos, and the penultimate features of a
classifier trained on those videos all converge on the same axis,
without any of them seeing the others' data. This is the Platonic
Representation Hypothesis~\citep{huh2024platonic} extended at
biological scale, in both directions: LLM-derived directions predict
brain responses, and brain-trained classifiers find the LLM-side
direction in return. Every brain claim replicates on SEED-V---most
sharply through a causal ablation whose effect is several times
larger than on FACED---so the convergence is not a peculiarity of one
recording.

The negative half of the story is the part with consequences. Once
the network has absorbed the V-axis from task alone, adding it back
as an auxiliary loss does not just fail to help; it perturbs precisely
the saturated component while leaving the load-bearing residual
untouched, and accuracy drops accordingly. Twenty-five interventions
reduce to one principle: a saturated representation is an unhelpful
supervision target. The same observation gives a positive
prescription: ensemble gain on a saturated network is variance
reduction in the orthogonal complement of the converged
subspace---generalising bias--variance \citep{geman1992neural} and
linear-mode connectivity \citep{frankle2020linearmode}---and that is
exactly what our $0.6948$/$0.6755$ FACED state of the art measures.

The regularity points beyond emotion. Most modern recipes for concept
supervision---structured knowledge distillation, representational
similarity, contrastive auxiliary losses, soft-prompt
projections---work by aligning a network's penultimate features with a
chosen concept geometry. Saturation gives those gains a horizon: they
sit in the regime where the task loss has not yet found the same
geometry on its own, and that regime shrinks as task supervision
strengthens. The dual prescription generalises in step. The only place
left to gain on a saturated network is the orthogonal complement of
its converged subspace, which is where ensembling, per-subject
adaptation, and any other variance-reduction strategy must live. We
expect both halves to recur wherever a concept direction is
recoverable from task supervision alone---including beyond valence in
LLMs, beyond emotion in EEG, and into vision-language models on
perceptual axes. Per-subject residual adaptation that lifts the
within-class signal without disturbing the basin is the immediate next
step (Appendix~\ref{app:discussion-extras},~\ref{app:negatives}).

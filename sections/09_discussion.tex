\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

A one-dimensional axis defined entirely from text via PCA on nine
emotion stories predicts five sentiment benchmarks at zero-shot AUC up
to $0.938$ (Yelp), 123-subject cohort EEG at $r{=}0.87$
($p<10^{-9}$), and EEG-classifier accuracy ($r{=}+0.885$ class-PC1,
$r{=}+0.74$ residual ensemble contribution, mean $z\approx 7.7\sigma$
causal-ablation closure). All five claims replicate on SEED-V (cohort
$r{=}+0.62$, posterior dominance, cross-arch $r{=}+0.601$, saturation,
causal closure $z{=}+23.6$). This extends Huh et~al.\
\citep{huh2024platonic} cross-modally: the same direction emerges in
LMs, brains, and EEG classifiers trained on those brains. Twenty-five
alignment interventions reduce to one principle: external alignment
cannot help once the model has absorbed the target from task alone
(transition sharp at FACED BACC $[0.62, 0.66]$). Ensemble gain on a
saturated network is variance reduction \emph{in the orthogonal
complement of the converged subspace}, generalising bias--variance
\citep{geman1992neural} and linear-mode connectivity
\citep{frankle2020linearmode} and yielding a new FACED 9-class SOTA
($0.6948$ / $0.6755$). Per-subject residual adaptation is the natural
next step (Appendix~\ref{app:discussion-extras},~\ref{app:negatives}).

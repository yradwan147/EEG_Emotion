\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

\subsection{Setup}

Unless otherwise noted, all experiments use \textbf{Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct}
\cite{qwen2.5} as the base LLM, extracting hidden states at layer 28 (the
final transformer block output). The nine stories per class come from the
pre-computed cache at
\texttt{line4/emotion\_vectors/qwen\_1.5B/stories\_*.json}.
For bootstrap confidence intervals, we use 1{,}000 resamples.
All datasets are loaded via the \texttt{datasets} library at the versions
current in April~2026.

\subsection{Zero-Shot Sentiment Classification}

the median (yielding a zero-label binary classifier). We also report a
calibrated threshold tuned on a held-out 200-sample subset.

\subsection{Word-Level Sentiment Lexicons}

\paragraph{Hu \& Liu 6{,}789 words.}
We extract the residual-stream embedding of each word in a minimal template
\texttt{"The word \textbackslash"\{w\}\textbackslash" means"}, then project onto
the V-axis.

\begin{table}[h]

\midrule

zero tightly ($[0.74, 0.78]$).

\paragraph{Continuous Sentence-Level Valence (EmoBank).}
EmoBank \cite{buechel2017emobank} provides 10{,}062 English sentences with
continuous VAD scores. Our V-axis projection correlates at
$|r|=\emobankr{}$ [0.485, 0.512] with ground-truth valence scores.
A supervised Ridge regression trained on the full feature space reaches
$|r|=0.66$; our method recovers $72\%$ of that signal with no labels.

\subsection{Fine-Grained Sentiment (SST-5)}

SST-5 provides 5-class ordinal valence (very negative \ldots very positive).
Our V-axis projection correlates at Pearson $r = 0.620$ (Spearman $0.627$)
with the 0--4 label on the 2{,}210-sample test set, confirming that the
axis captures continuous valence not just sign.

\paragraph{Functional alignment across models.}
Different models have different hidden-state dimensionalities, so we
cannot directly compare their V-axis \emph{vectors}. Instead, we
project the same 81 stories onto each model's V-axis, producing a
per-story valence score for every model. After a simple sign alignment
(positive emotions on the positive side), we compute pairwise cosine
similarity and Spearman rank correlation of these 81-dimensional
per-story valence vectors.

\begin{table}[h]

Cross-model mean pairwise cosine is $0.961$ and Spearman $\rho$ is
$0.954$, with a minimum of $0.937$ (Pythia-1.4b vs.\ Bloom-560m). The
four models --- two Qwen sizes, a GPT-NeoX model, and a BLOOM model ---
functionally agree on which of the 81 stories are more or less positive,
even though they have different architectures, training data, and
internal coordinate systems. This is stronger evidence for
universality than the per-model $|V_L|$ numbers in
\cref{tab:crossmodel}, which only show that each model individually has
a usable V-axis. The high cosine across language-model families
suggests that late-layer valence direction is a convergent solution of
the next-token prediction objective rather than an artifact of any
single training pipeline.

\subsection{PCA Beats Supervised LDA for OOD Transfer}

We compared two ways to extract the concept direction from the story
embeddings: (i)~PCA of the 9 centroids (our method, unsupervised);
(ii)~supervised linear discriminant analysis (LDA) on the per-story vectors
(450 samples, 9 classes).

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\caption{PCA beats LDA for OOD valence transfer.}
\label{tab:pca_vs_lda}
\begin{tabular}{lcc}
\toprule
Method & EmoBank V $|r|$ \\
\midrule
PCA (unsupervised, 9 centroids) & \textbf{0.498} \\
LDA (supervised, 450 per-story vectors) & 0.119 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

LDA overfits the 450 training stories and produces a direction that does not
transfer OOD. PCA, despite being unsupervised, is more robust.

\subsection{Honest Negative Results}

\paragraph{Concept generalization failure: toxicity.}
We attempted to generalize the method beyond valence by generating 15 toxic
and 15 safe stories, computing a T-axis as the centroid difference, and
testing on Jigsaw Civil Comments. Zero-shot AUC was 0.59, barely above
random. The method appears to be valence-specific. See Supplementary.

\paragraph{Multi-LLM ensemble hurts.}
Combining V-axes from Qwen-0.5B, Qwen-1.5B, Pythia-1.4B, and Bloom-560m
via sign-aligned mean projection underperforms the best single LLM
(Qwen-1.5B) by 0.02--0.04 on all three benchmarks. The cross-model
ensemble suffers from a weakest-link effect.

\paragraph{Prompting beats probing on raw accuracy.}
Direct ``is this positive or negative?'' prompting gives 92.78\% on SST-2,
15 percentage points higher than our probing. Our method's value is in
efficiency, interpretability, and continuous scores, not in raw classification
SOTA.

\section{Replication on a Second EEG Dataset (SEED-V)}
\label{sec:vaxis-brain-seedv}

A second EEG dataset, SEED-V~\citep{liu2022seedv}, lets us see whether
the same picture emerges when nothing is shared with FACED. We
re-build the V-axis from scratch on its five emotion classes on $16$
subjects and $62$ channels and re-test every FACED claim. The cohort
signal returns at $r{=}{+}0.6159$ ($p<10^{-5}$). The posterior gradient
returns. Cross-architecture convergence holds at $r{=}{+}0.601$
across $15$ stock CBraMod SEED-V checkpoints. Adding the V-axis as
supervision again does nothing measurable. Most strikingly, the causal
ablation replicates harder than on FACED: removing the V-axis residual
direction drops accuracy on every one of the $15$ SEED-V checkpoints
($\Delta\mathrm{BACC} \in [-0.029, -0.009]$) at mean
$z{=}{+}23.6$---roughly three times the FACED effect---with no
checkpoint surviving the null. Full numerics, FAA bars, and
per-checkpoint details are in Appendix~\ref{app:seedv-full}.

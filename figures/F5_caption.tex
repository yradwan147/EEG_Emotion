\textbf{The cohort V-axis effect is carried by a small set of high-arousal stimuli and washes out at the per-subject level.}
(a) Drop-class diagnostic: change in the all-28-stim cohort correlation when each emotion's stimuli are removed.
Removing Anger drops the cohort $r$ by $0.151$ ($32\%$ of the total effect, $p{<}0.001$); Amusement and Tenderness contribute another $\sim\!12\%$.
The effect is a 9-stim story, not a 28-stim story.
(b) Per-subject distribution at the cohort-best fixed channel set (PO3/$\gamma$ etc.): mean $r{=}{-}0.06$, indistinguishable from zero (Simpson's paradox).
The same data analyzed with an oracle channel-band selector per subject reaches mean $|r|{=}0.62$, suggesting a real per-subject signal that fixed channels cannot recover.
The cohort $r{=}0.48$ baseline (red dotted line) sits well above any single subject.
